UMD's Vietnam War Protest

The 1970s is when campus activity began to boom. When President Nixon decided to enter Cambodia during the Vietnam War, campus roared. According to The Washington Post on May 4, 1970, “[the protest is the] largest and most violent in the university’s history”. The Post reported about 2,000 students and 250 police officers in attendance. Route 1, a typical entrance to the university, was shut down by students. According to UMDarchives, students threw bricks, rocks, and bottles and slashed tires. The National Guard was brought in on several different occasions to disperse students. On the seventh day of protest, the governor issued an executive order to restrict access to University of Maryland’s campus.
